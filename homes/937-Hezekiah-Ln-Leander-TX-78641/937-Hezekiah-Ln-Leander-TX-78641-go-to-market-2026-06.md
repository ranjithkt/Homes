## 6. 🏆 The Recommendation (Balanced Objective)

### 📣 List at **$679,000** (~$191/sqft)

**Why this number:**
- ✅ It's the **top of the defensible value band** ($675K most-likely → $700K high), so it's a *strong* price — not a giveaway.
- ✅ It sits **below the $700K "knee,"** keeping expected DOM in the **~75–100 day** range (≈ area median) at **~98–100% sale-to-list**. That *is* the balanced objective: strong price at roughly market DOM.
- ✅ The psychological **"under $680K"** framing keeps the home inside the buyer search filter band that caps at $700K.
- ✅ Listing at $679K (vs the $665K quick-sale number) **funds a $10–15K buyer concession** while still netting ~$668–678K.

### 🧭 How the home's *qualitative* edge is used (positioning, NOT a price premium)
Per disciplined method, the East-facing/Vastu + multigen + 10/10-school stack does **not** add dollars on top of the comp-derived ceiling — it **widens the qualified-buyer pool and shortens DOM / firms negotiation at a given price.** So we deploy it as **marketing leverage and confidence**, not as a justification to list at $720K:
- It lets us hold the **top of the band ($679K)** with conviction that the right buyer exists.
- It supports a **tighter sale-to-list** (less give-back) than a generic listing at the same price.
- It argues for a **decisive single price move** (not a slow bleed) if the matched buyer hasn't surfaced in 3 weeks — because for *this* product, 3 weeks of full-exposure marketing should find the segment.

### 💳 Neutralize the builder financing gap
Builders are offering sub-6% + $20–25K. Counter with a **marketed "$10,000–$15,000 toward buyer's rate buydown or closing costs."** This converts the abstract "new homes are cheaper to finance" objection into an apples-to-apples monthly-payment match — funded by the strong list price, not by cutting it.

### ⏱️ The decision rule (pre-committed, removes emotion)
| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| **Days 0–21** | Hold **$679,000**. Full marketing blitz (Section 7). |
| **By Day 14** | If **<2 quality showings/week** or zero second-showings → prepare the cut. |
| **Day ~21–24** | If no accepted offer → **one decisive cut to $665,000** (~$187/sf). Re-blast as a "price improvement" to refresh search alerts. |
| **Day ~45** | If still unsold → revisit condition/staging/photography + consider $649K aggressive reset. |

This avoids the 1005-Hezekiah trap of chasing the market down $5K at a time over 11 months.
