### ⏳ The three DOM conventions (they're all "real" — they measure different things)

- 🟢 **Redfin "median days on market" = median days to *pending*** → the fast number (Snohomish **12**, 98272 **17**).
- 🟡 **Broker "average DOM"** (NWMLS, Madrona) → includes the slow long-tail → **24**.
- 🔴 **Realtor.com "median DOM" = median *active listing age*** → **35**.

So the honest blended baseline for an *average* Monroe home is **~12–17 days to pending, ~24–35 days of total market exposure.** But B is in the **$900K–$1.2M band**, where the math is different:

> 🎯 **DOM-by-band (B's $900K–$1.2M tier):** runs **~30–60 days of active exposure** — roughly **1.5–2.5× the all-price median** — and it's **bimodal, not linear.** A *well-priced, well-presented* home still pends in **~2–5 weeks**; a *mispriced* one sits **2–4+ months and takes cuts.** (Evidence: Home A unsold ~30–51 days at $996,900; one Eaglemont comp sat 109 days; the slow twins sat 116–237 days.)

### ☀️ July seasonality — small drag, manageable

- The Seattle-metro optimal listing window is **early April (+~2.9% premium)**; spring delivers the fastest DOM all year. **July is just past that peak** — the "summer doldrums": activity stays elevated but moderates; *"buyers have more options, buyers are more selective"* (Madrona, Jun 10).
- **Quantified drag:** ~**1–2% softer clearing price + a modest DOM uptick** vs an April listing — far better than fall/winter.
- 🎆 **Tactical July-4 nuance:** a *literal* July-1 list front-runs the July-4 holiday week (buyer traffic dips), wasting the valuable first-week "fresh listing" view spike. **→ List a few days *after* July 4.** Concrete DOM penalty for the launch timing: **+7–10 days** (baked into every scenario above).
