# Why Redfin & Zillow Price Two Same-Builder Monroe Homes ~$50K Apart — and Who Wants Each

*Macro/policy/market figures as-of **2026-06-21**.*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**The ~$50K gap is mostly an algorithmic illusion. Real value and real buyer demand both favor HOME A — the home both AVMs rank lower.**

Redfin and Zillow each value **HOME B above HOME A** — by **+$41,754** (Redfin: $1,028,922 vs $987,168) and **+$54,600** (Zillow: $1,032,700 vs $978,100). Yet every durable, buyer-relevant axis runs the *other* way: A is bigger (3,267 vs 3,238 sqft), newer (2017 vs 2016), **east-facing backing a greenbelt** (privacy/view, Vastu-favored) while B is west-facing backing houses, and A is staged "Very Good" with 40 photos against B's 24. A directional paradox exists: the homes' real attributes favor A, but the estimates favor B.

The resolution is **listing-status asymmetry**, not value. HOME A is **actively listed at $996,900 and not selling** — 51 cumulative days on market in a cooling 2026 Monroe market. That stale, rejected list price acts as a gravitational anchor, tethering A's AVM just *below* list and steering both models onto a conservative, lower comp set. HOME B is **off-market and unanchored**, so its estimate free-floats up onto a richer comp tile. One combined mechanism — a live, unsold listing dragging A while B floats — manufactures a gap larger than the headline, because it must first cancel A's genuine real-value edge and *then* push past it.

**Everything macro is common-mode and excluded from the differential.** Rates (~6.5%), the Snohomish conforming limit ($1,063,750), the Fed hold, the H-1B $100K-fee limbo, tariffs, SALT, broad Monroe cooling, and tech RSU/layoff/RTO dynamics set the **~$1M price LEVEL, the timing, and the buyer-pool health for BOTH homes equally** — they do not explain the A-vs-B delta. The single legitimate macro-differential bridge is the **asymmetric interaction**: active-A-vs-off-market-B *amplified by* the 2026 cooling, which is the same anchoring mechanism viewed through the macro lens, not a separate additive factor. **Bottom line: in a real, demand-cleared sale, HOME A would likely clear at or above HOME B.**

## 8. Per-Site Methodology (Zillow vs Redfin)

**Zillow's spread is $12,846 larger** than Redfin's (+$54,600 vs +$41,754).
Decomposed cleanly:
- On **A**: Redfin ($987,168) sits **+$9,068 above** Zillow ($978,100).
- On **B**: Zillow ($1,032,700) sits **+$3,778 above** Redfin ($1,028,922).
- $9,068 + $3,778 = **$12,846**.

Zillow builds the wider spread primarily by anchoring A lower. Against A's $996,900 non-selling list, Zillow applies a **−$18,800 (−1.89%)** discount versus Redfin's **−$9,732 (−0.98%)** — roughly double the downward haircut on a stale active listing. Zillow reaches a wider spread *despite* feeding data that favors A (A = 3 baths, B = 2 baths), proving that listing-status penalty and comp weighting completely overwhelm basic bedroom/bathroom/sqft multipliers.

## 10. Bottom Line / Implications

The ~$42K–$55K B-premium is **mostly an algorithmic illusion**. It is manufactured by a single mechanism: A's active, unsold listing anchoring its estimate down and onto low comps, while off-market B floats up. 

**In a real, demand-cleared sale, HOME A would likely clear at or above HOME B.** A is the bigger, newer, east-facing, greenbelt-backed, better-staged home with the deeper, higher-paying bid stack.

For A's seller, the active-unsold listing is itself depressing the AVM. At 51 DOM in a cooling market, the estimate is partly a self-inflicted artifact of being on-market-and-stale. For B's owner, the off-market AVM looks cleaner precisely because it is not being tested by today's selective buyer pool.
