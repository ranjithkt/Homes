# Investigation: The ~$48K Estimate Divergence Between Two Near-Adjacent Monroe Homes

**Homes:** A = 13506 196th Ave SE · B = 13668 198th Ave SE · Monroe WA 98272 · same builder (MainVue / Eaglemont) · ~1–2 blocks apart.

## 1. Executive Summary

Both Redfin and Zillow value **Home B above Home A** — Redfin by **+$41,754** ($1,028,922 vs $987,168) and Zillow by **+$54,600** ($1,032,700 vs $978,100), an average cross-home gap of **+$48,177** — even though Home A is newer (2017 vs 2016), marginally larger (3,267 vs 3,238 sqft), and actively listed at $996,900.

**This report corrects the prior conclusion.** The earlier report called the B-premium "mostly an algorithmic illusion," reasoning that Home A was the better-located home (it believed A was east-facing and backed a greenbelt). A vision re-extraction of the maps proved those spatial facts **wrong**. The corrected reality:

- **Orientation is a TIE — both homes face EAST.** `[MapA][MapB]`
- **Home A FRONTS a T-junction:** 135th St SE dead-ends into 196th Ave SE directly opposite A's entry — a road-thrust (Vastu *veedhi shoola* / feng-shui "poison arrow") plus headlight, noise, child-safety and privacy negatives; A's rear backs houses. `[MapA][PhotoA7]`
- **Home B BACKS a greenbelt / open green space** with a territorial Cascades view, and a community playground sits across the street. `[MapB][PhotoB7]`

So **location decisively favors B — which is exactly why both AVMs rank B higher.** The premium is **substantially REAL locational value**, not an illusion. Decomposed (common-mode-excluded), the average $48,177 gap splits **~$34,000 real value (71%)** — greenbelt/view and absence of A's road-thrust, net of A's small size/age edge — and **~$14,000 algorithmic (29%)** — Home A's active-unsold listing anchoring its estimate down plus comp-set divergence. An **independent demand-side estimate (+$40–50K central) brackets both AVMs**, corroborating that the gap is demand-real, not manufactured.

If anything, the **AVMs UNDERSTATE B's true edge**: algorithms price from structured fields and comps and have no input for "faces a T-junction" or "backs a greenbelt," so they capture those facts only indirectly. A human buyer or appraiser pricing the raw spatial superiority would likely put the gross gap at ~$50–80K — making the AVM gap a **conservative floor** on B's real-world advantage.

## 8. Per-Site Methodology

Zillow's gap (+**$54,600**) is **$12,846** wider than Redfin's (+**$41,754**).

- Zillow values Home A **$9,068 LOWER** than Redfin.
- Zillow values Home B **$3,778 HIGHER** than Redfin.

**Primary driver:** Comp weighting and list-anchoring treatment. Zillow applies a more aggressive Days-on-Market (DOM) depreciation penalty to Home A (51 cumulative DOM) and weights the active list price less strongly than Redfin does. Notably, this DOM penalty on A is powerful enough to override Zillow's own data feed penalizing Home B (it shows B at 2 baths instead of 2.5). The $13K cross-site spread is the methodology axis, not a different set of factors.
